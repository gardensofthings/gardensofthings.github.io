Gardens of Things

City gardens and urban green spaces power by sunlight and monitored by IoT sensors.

Gardens of Things is a research project of London's urban gardens and green spaces. We understand the environmental pressures of climate change and the future need for engineers. We are using Internet of Things devices and data science to monitor changes of urban green spaces and encourage the adoption of data-driven technologies to answer the challenges of air pollution, biodiversity loss, extreme weather and food insecurity.

This research will benefit green infrastructures in cities to help tackle climate change and food system resilience and assist digital education. We are reinventing the way citizens perceive our green spaces and are offering support to solve tomorrow challenges.

Smart Technology

Internet of Things

The application of smart technologies will foster a thriving and resilient biodiverse urban system. We are deploying sensors to capture, measure and evaluate key aspects of green habitats and will provide gardeners, scientists and any member of the public insights into the surrounding habitats. 
‍
Soil sensors, weather stations, cameras and infrared sensors will provide real-time data on every elements of a garden. Soil temperature and humidity, sunlight and rain, air pollution and wind speed, insects and birds population, or even plant growth will be tracked.  

Green Spaces

Urban Gardens

Urban green spaces provide rooms for flowers to bloom, habitats for insects to live and place for human to meet. Rooftops, green walls, schoolyards, street trees, urban gardens and others have all the potential to become the city's biodiverse oasis!  
‍
We are developing local, experimental and participatory projects to test the design, performance and use of urban gardens and green spaces. We are collaborating with urban communities, scientists and agencies to understand the benefits of nature on people and the environment.

Biodiversity

The project will use cutting-edge sensors to capture the living organisms populating urban green spaces. Combining cameras, ultrasonic microphones and deep learning algorithms, various insects and birds species will be identify to assess the biodiversity of the different urban gardens.

City Resilience

The project will built on the London Mayor's Food Strategy and City Resilience Strategy. Engaging with diverse stakeholders (engineers, gardeners, architects and teachers), the research aims to provide novel understandings of how green spaces help to manage shocks, such as drought and flooding, and stresses, such as air quality, food insecurity, and poor health. This will engage the three pillars of a resilient city: people, place and process.

Digital Education

Gardens of Things aims to provide young people with computing and digital making skills. Partnering with schools, it will allow children to experiment with Internet of Things and discover a novel way to engage with their natural environment. This will provide them with both an appreciation for their natural environment as well as necessary and relevant computing skills for the future of work.
